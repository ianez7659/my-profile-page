## Thinking About Frontend Beyond the Browser

When I first started learning frontend development, I naturally thought of it as something that lived entirely inside the browser.
Building interfaces, handling interactions, and making things look and feel right on the screen felt like the core of the job.

As I continued learning and working on small projects, however, I began to notice that this view was a bit too narrow.
Frontend development seemed to touch more areas than I initially expected, and the boundaries between web, native, and even server-side concerns felt less clear than I had assumed.

## Closing Thoughts

From my point of view, the idea that frontend boundaries are blurring is not intimidating—it is motivating.  It does not mean that frontend has become unclear or unfocused.  Instead, it suggests that the role has more depth and influence than I initially expected.

As the lines between web, native, and server continue to blur, I find myself thinking less about individual tools and more about how experiences are structured and delivered as a whole.
For me, that shift has become an important part of how I now think about frontend development.
